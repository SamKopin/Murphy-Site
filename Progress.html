Project Murphy has been hard at work for two semesters. Trying to create the little robot Murphy. At the start, there were 2 concept designs.

CAD stands for Computer-aided design. There's programs where you can design in 2D and turn it into a 3D piece. With these blueprint's you can build things for them either with 3D printing or simply have a blueprint to work off of. Either way CAD is a very useful program, especially when it comes to building Murphy.

The Raspberry Pi CAD model and the chip itself. They're a signle-board computer that are inexpensive chips. Mainly used in building PCs, they have plenty of other uses such as building Murphy here. It only takes about 5-7 watts of electricity and has an SD card storage, making this an easy to use tool for our robot friend.

While it's been going well, there's also struggles and fails along the way. That's the case when trying to build a robot. It's trial and error, and in project Murphy it's gone from fails with 3D printers to parts breaking and needing to recut or print.
